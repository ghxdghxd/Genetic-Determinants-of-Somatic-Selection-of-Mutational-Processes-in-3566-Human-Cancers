    Genetic Determinants of the Somatic Selection of Mutational Processes in 3,566 Human Cancers

    Abstract

    The somatic landscape of cancer genome is a result of the activities of different mutational processes, which are represented by distinct mutational signatures. Although several mutagenic mechanisms are known to cause specific mutational signatures in cell lines, the variation of the somatic mutational activities in patients, which is mostly attributed to the somatic selection, is still poorly explained. Here, we described an integrated method to suggest genetic determinants of the variations in the mutational processes in 3,566 cancers; and inferred the underlying selection processes by applying genetic instrumental variables. As a result, we report 2,314 candidate determinants with both significant germline and somatic effects on the somatic selection of mutational processes, of which 485 acts via cancer expression; and 1,427 acts through interactions with tumor immune microenvironment, for which we further evaluated the clinicopathological relevance. The findings of our study provide new clues to understand clonal evolution and intra-and inter-tumor heterogeneity which further inform the precision medicine for cancer.
